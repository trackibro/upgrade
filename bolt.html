🧬 Science Lab — The Physiology of BOLT 50
🧠 CO₂ Tolerance Model
BOLT measures CO₂ sensitivity of central chemoreceptors in the medulla oblongata. Low BOLT = chemoreceptors trigger breathing at low CO₂ (hypersensitivity).
Bohr Effect: ↑CO₂ → ↑H⁺ → hemoglobin releases O₂
Low BOLT → low PaCO₂ → O₂ stays bound to Hb → tissue hypoxia
Training → ↑PaCO₂ setpoint → ↑O₂ delivery efficiency
Every 5s BOLT increase = measurably better tissue oxygenation.
📈 Adaptation Timeline
Week 1
Neural phase: Brain learns breath-hold isn't dangerous. +1–2s.
Week 2–4
Chemoreceptor recalibration: +3–5s. Most early gains here.
Week 4–8
Structural adaptation: Blood buffering capacity increases. +5–10s.
Week 8–16
Systemic integration: Breathing permanently more efficient. BOLT 40+.
Week 16–24
Elite range: BOLT 50+ achievable with full protocol.
